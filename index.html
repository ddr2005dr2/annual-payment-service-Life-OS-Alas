Security and trust

The platform should communicate visible trust architecture across the front end, middle layer, and backend so users understand the product is secure and deliberate.

Encryption

Design target: TLS 1.3 for data in transit, AES-256 at rest, encrypted backups, and KMS-managed key controls.

Access control

Least-privilege permissions, MFA-capable authentication, server-side secret handling, and audited administrative actions.

User safety

Minimal permission requests, plain-language disclosures, visible policy pages, and deletion and cancellation pathways that are easy to understand.

Frequently asked questions

These answers should be deliberate, strong, and written to reduce hesitation while staying truthful about what the product does and does not do.

What does LifeOS Atlas actually help with?

It helps users organize reminders, obligations, deadlines, bills, routines, and guided next steps in one product layer so fewer important things slip through the cracks.

Is the concierge a human?

No. The concierge is an AI-guided product concierge designed to help with LifeOS Atlas questions, onboarding, workflows, and support topics only.

Will subscriptions be active immediately?

No. Entry can remain free while the business evaluates traction. Other lawful monetization channels may operate before recurring subscriptions are switched on.

How is data protected?

The platform is intended to use strong encryption, strict access control, minimal-permission integrations, and transparent privacy and deletion controls.

Can I cancel later?

Yes. Users should be able to cancel future paid plans before renewal. Cancellation stops future recurring billing, but it does not create a refund for digital services already delivered.

Do you offer refunds?

For digital services, the policy can state that fulfilled digital access, configuration, or delivered service is non-refundable. That policy should be shown clearly before purchase.
